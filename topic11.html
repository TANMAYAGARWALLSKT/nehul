INTRODUCTION TO CTS PIONEERS

We were introduced to two pioneers of CTS:

TONY BUZAN

Tony Buzan was a renowned author, speaker, and educational consultant, best known for his work on mind mapping and memory techniques. He revolutionized the field of learning and cognitive psychology with his innovative methods for enhancing creativity, productivity, and memory retention. Through his numerous books and lectures, Buzan inspired millions to unlock their full potential by harnessing the power of their minds. His legacy continues to influence educators, students, and professionals worldwide.

EDWARD DE BONO

Edward de Bono, a Maltese physician, psychologist, and author, is best known for his work in the field of creative thinking. Born in 1933, deBono has written over 60 books, which have been translated into 37 languages. He is the originator of the term "lateral thinking," which describes a thinking style that seeks to solve problems through an indirect and creative approach. De Bono's work has had a profound impact on fields such as education, business, and psychology, challenging conventional thinking patterns and promoting innovative solutions. His books, including "Six Thinking Hats" and "Lateral Thinking," continue to be influential in encouraging individuals and organizations to think more creatively and effectively
