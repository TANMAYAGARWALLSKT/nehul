MORE THAN MEETS THE EYE

The Rubin face or the figure–ground vase is a famous example of ambiguous or bi-stable (i.e., reversing) two-dimensional forms developed around 1915 by the Danish psychologist Edgar Rubin. An average person sees only two things in this image i.e either two faces or a vase. But, the one who thinks different may see a variety of things. Heres what we saw, Vine glass, Stool, Bird Bath, Grip of a sword, Cauldron, candle holder, chess piece, etc… Were you able to Visualize our perspective?
