CATWOE

Introduction: In this class, we delve into the powerful framework of CATWOE, a conceptual tool designed by Peter Checkland as part of Soft Systems Methodology.

CATWOE stands for Customers, Actors, Transformation, Weltanschauung (Worldview), Owners, and Environmental Constraints.

This framework serves as a lens through which we can comprehensively analyze and understand complex organizational dynamics.

CUSTOMERS - Identify and analyze the primary beneficiaries or stakeholders in a system or problem.

ACTORS - Explore the roles and responsibilities of individuals or entities involved in the processes.

TRANSFORMATION - Examine the processes and activities that convert inputs into desired outputs within an organizational context.

WORLDVIEW - Delve into the underlying beliefs, values, and assumptions that influence how individuals perceive and interact with the system or problem.

OWNERS - Investigate the entities or individuals who own and wield authority over the system, decision-making processes, or key aspects of the organization.

ENVIRONMENTAL CONSTRAINTS - Consider the external factors and limitations that impact the system, exploring how organizations navigate and adapt to their broader context.
